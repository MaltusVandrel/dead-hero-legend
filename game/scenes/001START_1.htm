"Sea is a strange thing, it's alive, breathing and pulsing. More than that! It's a jealous entity, taking and ripping apart everything it wants with waves like titan arms, thundering screams, and greedy pulls from the everpresent currents; Yet... It's a lovable thing, a nurturing thing, fostering life and dreams, promising adventures and home, calling like a siren and shining beauty like a nymph. Sometimes even, over all its greed, giving gifts to their lovers and suitors;"

"Maybe that's why I drifted there with all of my unconscious hope. — Good to know that my heart still dreams."

"Nothing but beauty for so long, the time is such of a meaningless thing for me that I can't even count waves anymore — If I could, maybe I would be crazy at this point; Or would I be saner?"

"Then, so expected but of no short of surprise and happiness, I found such a sad thing."

"A drowned body, a hopeless grey thing; for now."

"Be back to what you were, raise dear unknown but soon to be friend!"

A body, half-decomposed, half-eaten, returned to a healthy, and more surprisingly, an alive state with its true soul flowing back from the ocean grip to its rightful owner.

It floated slowly and surely, pulled by the watcher force, unconscious yet protected.

"Let's see what I caught."

The watcher inspected the floating one, permeating the peaceful unaware continuousness.

The undrowned sensed a disembodied will, much like a voice but without sound, compelling to answer:
